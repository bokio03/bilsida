Let's introduce the BMW M5

The German Dream

Stats:

  • 4.4-liter V-8 with 625 horsepower
  • Acceleration, 0-100 km/h - 3.3sek
  • Top speed - 315 km/h

The BMW M5, a flagship model within the BMW 5 Series, proudly carries the prestigious BMW M sub-brand. Revered as an iconic representation in the sports sedan category, the M5 has primarily been manufactured in the sedan (saloon) body style. However, it's interesting to note that, in select regions, this exceptional vehicle has also been made available as an estate (wagon) model during specific time periods. This diverse range of body styles underlines the M5's adaptability to varied market demands.

Let's introduce the BMW M4

The German Beast

Stats:

  • 3.0-liter Twin-turbocharged engine with 506 horsepower
  • Acceleration, 0-100 km/h - 3.9sek
  • Top speed - 307km/h

The BMW M4 is a high-performance model derived from the BMW 4 Series and is part of the prestigious BMW M sub-brand. It has earned a well-deserved reputation as an iconic sports coupe in the automotive world. While the BMW M4 is primarily produced in the coupe and convertible body styles, it's worth noting that in certain regions, such as Europe, it has been available as a sedan (saloon) variant from 2020 onwards. This versatility in body styles allows BMW enthusiasts to choose a model that suits their preferences and needs.

The M4 is a highway star, for sure, but unlike the wagon – it can't just blow anyone away, but it offers precision at high speeds and stability.

On 25 September 2013, BMW released the technical specifications of the M4. It is powered by the S55B30 engine, which is developed and engineered by BMW M GmbH.

So, whether you seek heart-pounding speed or crave a tech-savvy driving experience, the BMW M4 is here to deliver.
